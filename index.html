Quick Answer: Why Track Productivity?

A productivity calculator turns billing rate, hours, and headcount into the revenue numbers leadership actually cares about. Team-utilization mode multiplies team size × hours × billable % × billing rate × 4.333 weeks per month to get monthly revenue at your current and target utilization. The gap is what tracking would unlock.

Revenue mode divides total revenue by employees and by total team hours to give you revenue per person and per hour. Agencies average 55–60% utilization; a healthy services target is 70–80%.

That number isn’t a target; it’s the revenue your tracking is currently failing to capture.

The Productivity Formulas Explained

Team utilization → monthly revenue
• Monthly billable hours = Team size × Hours/week × (Utilization% ÷ 100) × 4.333
• Monthly revenue = Monthly billable hours × Billing rate
• Monthly gap = Revenue at target − Revenue at current

Revenue productivity
• Revenue per employee = Total annual revenue ÷ Number of employees
• Total annual hours = Employees × Hours/week × 52
• Revenue per hour = Total annual revenue ÷ Total annual hours

Common Mistakes With Productivity Calculators

  • Targeting 100% utilization: Nobody hits 100% sustainably. Internal meetings, training, and PTO take real time. 70–80% is the honest target.
  • Confusing total hours with billable hours: If you only enter billable hours, the gap collapses to zero. The formula expects total tracked hours.
  • Using an outdated blended rate: A blended rate from two years ago will under-report the gap.
  • Comparing per-employee revenue across industries: A $500k/employee SaaS number isn’t a target for an agency that does heavy human delivery.
