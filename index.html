What Is the Productivity Calculator?

The productivity calculator on this page is a free, browser-based tool that runs the two productivity calculations most agencies, consultancies, and operators actually need:

  • Team Utilization Mode: Takes your team size, blended billing rate, hours per week, and your current versus target utilization percentages, and returns the monthly revenue you’d unlock by closing the gap.
  • Revenue Productivity Mode: Asks for total revenue, headcount, and weekly hours per person, then quietly multiplies by 52 weeks in the background so you never have to do annual-hours math in your head.

Both modes run entirely in your browser, auto-detect your currency from your timezone, and never upload your numbers to a server. Most agencies that try a productivity calculator for the first time are surprised to find that a 35-point utilization gap (from 45% to 80%) across a five-person team at $125/hr is roughly $37,888 of unbilled revenue every month.

How the Productivity Calculator Works

Both modes are deliberately simple. In Team utilization mode, the calculator multiplies your team size by hours per week, by utilization, by 4.333 weeks per month (52 ÷ 12), and by billing rate. In Revenue productivity mode, it just divides total annual revenue by headcount for revenue per employee.

The Productivity Formulas Explained

Team Utilization → Monthly Revenue
Monthly billable hours = Team size × Hours/week × (Utilization% ÷ 100) × 4.333
Monthly revenue = Monthly billable hours × Billing rate
Monthly gap = Revenue at target − Revenue at current

Revenue Productivity
Revenue per employee = Total annual revenue ÷ Number of employees
Total annual hours = Employees × Hours/week × 52
Revenue per hour = Total annual revenue ÷ Total annual hours

Utilization Benchmarks Worth Knowing

Raw utilization percentages are easier to read against industry benchmarks. The bands below mirror the thresholds SPI Research publish in their annual professional-services reports.

Billable Utilization What it usually means Typical for
Under 40% Tracking is broken — most billable time isn’t being captured. Newly tracked teams, week one
40% – 55% Below average. Real revenue is being lost to unbilled work. Smaller agencies pre-tracking
55% – 60% Industry average for professional-services agencies. Typical mid-sized agency
60% – 75% Healthy. Most billable hours are being captured and billed. Well-run consultancies
75% – 85% High performance. Common for senior billable roles at top firms. Top-quartile services firms
Above 85% Risk zone — usually unsustainable without burnout. Rarely a steady-state target

How to Use the Productivity Calculator

  2. Confirm or change the currency: The tool auto-detects from your browser timezone (PKR, USD, GBP, EUR, etc.).
  3. Enter team size, billing rate, and hours: Type the headcount, your blended hourly rate, and standard hours per week.

Common Mistakes With Productivity Calculators

  • Targeting 100% utilization: Nobody hits 100% sustainably. 70–80% is the honest target.
  • Confusing total hours with billable hours: The utilization formula expects total tracked hours in the denominator and billable hours in the numerator.
  • Using an outdated blended rate: Refresh the rate whenever a senior role joins or leaves to get accurate data.
